ELEVEN AS ONE: Generalized Interior Design of the PSTMIS SHS Reading Corner Using Grade 11 Students' Project Proposals

Creating a comfortable and well-designed reading area encourages students to read more, improving their literacy skills, which are crucial in today's information-driven era. Interior design plays a key role in setting the mood and creating a functional, inviting atmosphere that motivates students to visit and engage in reading or studying. This project aims to develop a safe, comfortable, and aesthetically pleasing space for studying, reading, entertainment, and research. A well-organized environment fosters positive feelings, boosts mood and concentration, and ensures safety, making it a valuable initiative for enhancing learning and personal growth.

Improvised Sofa Cushion with Open Shelving made of Mulawin Wood and Plastic Wastes

Having a reading space in schools is essential for enhancing students' literacy, vocabulary, imagination, and creativity. However, not all schools can afford large reading areas. The "Improvised Sofa Cushion with Open Shelving" project addresses this challenge by using sustainable materials like durable Mulawin wood and plastic waste. The sofa is designed for comfort in small spaces, with built-in shelving for book storage, promoting neatness and convenience. Using recycled plastic reduces costs, supports environmental conservation, and repurposes household waste, making the project cost-effective and eco-friendly.
